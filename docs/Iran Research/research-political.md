# Political and domestic dynamics for the US-Israel-Iran conflict simulation

The 2026 Iran War — now in its **19th day** — has triggered the most significant geopolitical rupture since the 2003 Iraq invasion, with verified consequences spanning assassinated heads of state, the largest oil supply disruption in history, and a fracturing of the US-led Middle Eastern security architecture. Public opposition in the United States started higher than for any comparable conflict, Gulf allies are reviewing trillions in US-linked investments, and Iran's decentralized military command is functioning despite unprecedented leadership decapitation. This report verifies, corrects, and quantifies each political dynamic across the four primary actors.

### How long from opposition to policy change: the historical gap

📊 **Vietnam:** Majority opposition reached by **~1967**. Nixon elected 1968 on promise to end war. Paris Peace Accords signed January 1973. Last troops left April 1975. **Gap: ~6–8 years.** Mechanisms: elections, anti-war movement, draft resistance, media pressure, economic strain. War Powers Resolution passed in 1973 as a *result* of Vietnam.

📊 **Iraq:** Majority called war "a mistake" by **mid-2004** (~15 months after invasion). Democrats took Congress in 2006 midterms. Congress passed withdrawal deadlines in 2007 (Bush vetoed). Status of Forces Agreement signed 2008 with December 2011 deadline. Full withdrawal December 2011. **Gap: ~7 years.** Mechanisms: 2006 midterms, 2008 presidential election, cumulative casualties, no WMD found.

➕ **Critical difference for Iran:** The Iran War starts with **lower support than either Vietnam or Iraq at inception**. Americans are less tolerant of casualties (60%+ say unacceptable vs. 58% acceptable in Iraq after week 1). There is no equivalent of post-9/11 trauma driving support. If historical patterns hold, political consequences could arrive **faster** — potentially as early as the November 2026 midterms.

## 2. Iran: decapitated but not defeated

### Supreme Leader succession

✅ **Verified: Ayatollah Ali Khamenei was killed February 28, 2026** in joint US-Israeli strikes on his compound in central Tehran. Iranian state media confirmed his death March 1 after initially denying it. CIA had tracked his movements and shared intelligence with Israel. Also killed: Defense Minister Nasirzadeh, IRGC Commander-in-Chief Pakpour, intelligence chief Asadi, and other senior figures.

✅ **Verified: Mojtaba Khamenei was elected third Supreme Leader on March 8–9, 2026** by the Assembly of Experts. The constitutional process was formally followed — the Assembly convened from March 3–8, and Mojtaba obtained the required two-thirds majority in the initial round.

⚠️ **However, his current status is deeply uncertain.** He was reportedly injured in the February 28 strikes. His wife, sister, and mother were killed. US Defense Secretary Hegseth stated he is "wounded and likely disfigured." **No video or audio of Mojtaba has been released since the war began** — his first message (March 12) was read by a state TV anchor over a still photo. On March 16, reports emerged he was transported to Moscow on a Russian military plane. The White House assesses Iran is "essentially leaderless" with the IRGC calling the shots.

📊 **Mojtaba's profile:** Age 56, born in Mashhad. Joined IRGC at 17; served in Iran-Iraq War. Mid-level clerical rank of Hojjatoleslam (NOT an Ayatollah) — a significant legitimacy deficit under Iran's Velayat-e Faqih system. No published jurisprudential works. **Took control of the Basij paramilitary in 2009** and is blamed by reformists for suppressing the Green Movement. Sanctioned by US Treasury in 2019. Described by Carnegie's Karim Sadjadpour as "more radical" than his father. His father reportedly considered him "not very bright" and "unqualified" per US intelligence assessments. Washington Institute analysis assesses he is **more favorable to nuclear weapons development** than his father.

⚠️ **Succession legitimacy issues:** Ali Khamenei had **not named his son** as a potential successor — his three nominees were Mohseni-Ejei, Asghar Hijazi, and Hassan Khomeini. Iran International reported the IRGC pressured Assembly members through "repeated contacts and psychological and political pressure." Israel and the US actively threatened to target any successor, further distorting the process.

### The January 2026 massacre

✅ **Independently verified by Amnesty International, Human Rights Watch, and UN bodies** as one of the deadliest protest crackdowns in modern history. The crackdown followed massive nationwide protests that erupted December 28, 2025 over currency collapse and economic crisis, spreading to all **31 provinces and 200+ cities**.

The UN Special Rapporteur concluded the evidence "justified investigating the possibility that the killings were crimes against humanity." Ali Khamenei personally ordered to "crush the protests by any means necessary."

### Iranian public sentiment: divided, not unified

⚠️ **No reliable polling exists inside wartime Iran.** Best assessment draws from pre-war surveys, verified footage, and credible reporting:

- **Pre-war (GAMAAN 2024):** ~70% opposed continuation of Islamic Republic; 89% supported democracy; 65% planned election boycott
- **Post-strikes:** Some Iranians **celebrated Khamenei's death** in Isfahan, Karaj, Kermanshah, Shiraz, and other cities — filming celebrations and toppling a Khamenei statue in Dehloran. Massive diaspora celebrations: 350,000 in Los Angeles, 350,000 in Toronto, 250,000+ in Munich
- **Pro-government mourning rallies** also occurred in Tehran with thousands gathering
- **Carnegie Endowment** assessed that any rally-around-the-flag effect from the June 2025 war "rapidly dissipated" before the current conflict even began
- The regime is using wartime conditions to **suppress all dissent as treason**, threatening execution for anti-establishment activity. The primary dynamic is **repression, not unity**

⚠️ **Propaganda vs. reality:** Iranian state media frames all protesters as "Zionist and American agents." US and Israeli sources emphasize celebration footage. The reality is a deeply fractured society where genuine nationalist sentiment coexists with profound anti-regime anger — but the regime holds power through force, not consent.

### Command structure resilience

✅ **Iran's "Mosaic Defense" — decentralized by design and functioning.** Under restructuring completed by 2019, the IRGC was reorganized into **31 semi-autonomous provincial commands**, each with independent intelligence, weapons stockpiles, and pre-delegated authority to launch strikes if communication with the center is severed.

📊 **Evidence it works:** Iranian FM Araghchi admitted on March 1: "Our military units are now, in fact, independent and somewhat isolated, and they are acting based on general instructions given to them in advance." Despite killing the Supreme Leader, IRGC commander-in-chief, defense minister, and other senior figures on Day 1, Iran's military response continued and expanded — launching retaliatory strikes against US bases in **9 countries** and effectively closing the Strait of Hormuz.

➕ **Pre-war US intelligence assessment:** A classified report stated that large-scale war on Iran would be unlikely to produce regime change because the Islamic Republic is "fundamentally different from highly personalised autocracies" — it prioritizes system preservation over any individual leader. Ahmad Vahidi was named new IRGC commander-in-chief (appointed deputy in December 2025 specifically to prepare for possible attack). Washington Post (March 16) reports US intelligence assesses the regime "will likely remain in place for now, weakened but more hard-line, with the IRGC exerting greater control."

### Domestic opposition: fragmented and unable to capitalize

⚠️ **No organized opposition inside Iran is capable of filling a power vacuum** (Time, March 16). The exile opposition is "diverse but deeply fragmented" with competing claims of leadership. Reza Pahlavi (exiled crown prince) is the most visible figure but his actual support inside Iran is unknown. The MEK/NCRI is deeply unpopular inside Iran due to siding with Iraq in the 1980s. No security force defections have been reported. Despite Trump's call to "take over your government," there is little sign of regime-toppling street action during wartime.

## 4. Gulf States: betrayed by allies, attacked by neighbors

✅ **Verified: Iran launched retaliatory strikes against UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait, and even neutral Oman.** Over **2,000 missiles and drones** were launched at Gulf states; approximately 1,800 intercepted, with hundreds breaking through.

## Simulation-ready political dynamics summary

### United States
- **War support:** 37–44% (average ~41%), lower than any comparable US conflict at inception
- **Partisan gap:** 83 points (90% MAGA R support vs. 7% D support)
- **Congressional constraint:** War Powers resolutions failed by 6 votes (Senate) and 7 votes (House). No AUMF. GOP holds but narrowly.
- **Elite fracture index:** High — Carlson, Greene, Kent, Walsh, Rogan form significant right-flank opposition. Kent resignation = highest-profile admin defection.
- **Midterm vulnerability:** Pre-war models project R losing ~28 House seats. War compounds with economic anxiety, gas prices ($3.60+), base fracture.
- **Historical policy-change lag:** Vietnam ~6–8 years, Iraq ~7 years from majority opposition. Iran starts with lower support — political consequences may arrive at 2026 midterms (~8 months).
- **Israel lobby spending capacity:** ~$127M/cycle, reaching 65% of Congress. But retreating from 2026 cycle spending amid backlash.

### Iran
- **Leadership status:** Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei elected but possibly incapacitated (injured, possibly in Moscow). IRGC effectively governing.
- **Military resilience:** Mosaic Defense functioning — 31 autonomous provincial commands operating on pre-delegated authority. Retaliatory capacity continuing at Day 19. Hormuz closure = primary strategic leverage.
- **Regime stability:** Weakened but holding through force. No security force defections. IRGC consolidating power. US intelligence assesses regime "will likely remain."
- **Public sentiment:** Deeply divided. Anti-regime sentiment (70% pre-war) coexists with some nationalist rally effect. Primary mechanism of control: repression, not consent.
- **Opposition capacity:** Fragmented, exile-based, unable to fill power vacuum. No organized internal force capable of regime change.
