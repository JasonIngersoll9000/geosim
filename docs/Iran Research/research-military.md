## SECTION 4: Nuclear status

### Khamenei's fatwa against nuclear weapons

⚠️ **The fatwa's existence and binding nature were always disputed.** The Atlantic Council assessed that "Khamenei never issued a fatwa against building a nuclear weapon" — what existed was a paragraph from a 2010 message to a conference, not a formal religious decree. Iran's own official newspaper argued the fatwa only bans **use**, not **production or stockpiling**. In Shia jurisprudence, a fatwa is tied to the life of the issuing marja. Since Khamenei never codified it as a governmental decree, **it effectively died with him on February 28, 2026.** His successor Mojtaba Khamenei has neither reaffirmed nor rejected it.

### Are both constraints on nuclear breakout removed?

✅ **The user's logic is sound.** The two primary political/strategic constraints on Iranian nuclear weapons development were: (1) the fatwa/supreme leader's prohibition, and (2) the calculation that pursuing weapons would invite military strikes. With Khamenei dead (removing constraint 1) and strikes already having occurred (removing constraint 2), **both barriers have been eliminated**. Former defense minister Ali Shamkhani publicly stated in October 2025: "If I returned to the defence portfolio, I would move toward building an atomic bomb."

### Iran's current breakout timeline

📊 **The situation is deeply uncertain, which is itself the central danger:**

**Fissile material production:** If Iran can access its **440 kg of 60% enriched uranium** (enough for ~10 weapons if enriched to 90%) and has even a small number of advanced centrifuges, enriching from 60% to weapons-grade 90% would take **"a few weeks"** (IAEA Director Grossi, September 2025). A relatively small number of IR-6 centrifuges would suffice.

**Weaponization:** Producing a deliverable nuclear weapon would require **months to a year** beyond having weapons-grade uranium (per Center for Arms Control analysis).

**Critical unknown:** The IAEA **"cannot provide any information on the current size, composition or whereabouts"** of Iran's enriched uranium stockpile (February 27, 2026 report). IAEA assesses ~200 kg of the 60% material is underground at Isfahan; the remainder's location is uncertain. Iran has had no IAEA inspections at damaged sites since June 2025 and formally suspended cooperation in July 2025.

### Centrifuge capacity and reconstitution

All declared enrichment sites (Fordow, Natanz, Isfahan) are damaged/inoperable. **However:** Since the IAEA lost centrifuge manufacturing verification in 2021, "it is credible that Iran could already have one or more cascades of IR-6 centrifuges hidden in an unknown location" (Center for Arms Control, March 2026). Iran is actively constructing **Pickaxe Mountain** — an underground site near Natanz, buried up to 100 meters beneath a mountain taller than Fordow's, that may be impervious to aerial bombardment. Satellite imagery shows Iran is "accelerating work" and potentially transferring centrifuges and equipment rescued from other sites. A separate covert site, **Minzadehei** (northeast of Tehran), was struck by Israel after intelligence indicated Iran was relocating nuclear weapons-related infrastructure there.

**Phase 1 — The Twelve-Day War (June 13–24, 2025):** Israel initiated with a massive surprise attack on Iran's military and nuclear infrastructure. Economic crisis triggered massive protests (January 2026); regime killed thousands of its own citizens. Diplomatic breakthrough announced February 27 — Iran agreed to full nuclear verification. US conducted massive military buildup, largest since 2003.

- **Cost asymmetry is real and acute:** Iran spends $35K per drone; defenders spend $3.7M–$15M per interceptor. US interceptor stocks could be exhausted within weeks at current consumption. Production cannot keep pace.
